Bucket List of Questions

This is a list of questions that I have either no answer to or no satisfactory answer to. Every so often I will try to work on a couple of them. I refuse to look them up (in some cases that's not even possible because the problem is with my own understanding of the material) but if you have a hint please let me know.
  1. Is every group the fundamental group of a topological space?
    • A completely delightful question posed to me by two different people. Of all the questions here, this is the one that eats at me the most.
  2. Is there a simple description for subsets $A$ of $\mathbb{R}^n$ where $\text{Bd}(\text{Bd}(A)) = \text{Bd}(A)$?
    • Somehow feels like these should be 'nice' subsets since counterexamples seem to be subsets with lots of holes in them (e.g. $\mathbb{Q}$).
  3. Why is there no natural isomorphism from a finite dimensional vector space to its dual?
    • I know that the answer lies somewhere in category theory. I don't know enough to know how.
  4. What does it mean to say that the $K_0$ group of a field is the integers? How exactly does it relate to dimensions of a vector space?
    • One of those cases where I can do the computation but I don't know what it means. This frustrates me.
  5. Up to homeomorphism, there is a unique topological space that is non-empty, totally disconnected, perfect, compact and metrizable (the Cantor set). How far can we weaken the conditions so that uniqueness no longer holds?
  6. Does there exist a group $G$ such that $\text{Aut}(G)$ is trivial?

Answered

  1. Suppose $G, H$ are groups such that $G \times H \cong G$. Does this imply that $H$ is trivial?
    • No. Suppose $G$ is a group. Then we can define $G_\infty := \prod_{i = 1}^\infty G = \{f: \mathbb{N} \to G\}$. We can make this a group by defining the operation pointwise. We then observe that $G_\infty \times G = G_\infty$.
